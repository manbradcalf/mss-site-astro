Where it breaks

The agents are the easy part. The system around them isn't.

Spinning up an agent is a weekend. Getting several to coordinate, hand off cleanly, remember, and not run up a bill nobody planned for, that's where these projects stall and quietly die before production.

01 / coordination

Hand-offs break

Agents that can't pass work cleanly stall the moment a task spans more than one step. The orchestration is the actual engineering.

02 / cost

Spend with nothing to show

Without a design, agents loop, retry, and burn tokens. Gartner pins runaway cost and unclear value as the top reasons these projects get killed.

03 / control

No guardrails

Autonomous agents with no limits, memory, or human checkpoint do confident, expensive, wrong things.
